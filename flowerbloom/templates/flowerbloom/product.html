Product Description

When it comes to Tulips, people often think of the beautiful country of the Netherlands because Tulips are a distinctive symbol of the Netherlands. However, Tulips actually originate from the ancient lands of Persia and Turkey.

The illustrious history of Tulips reaches far corners of the world, associating it with many powerful symbols. Tulips represent fame, wealth, and perfect love. Perhaps because they bloom in spring, erasing the darkness of winter days, Tulips become a symbol of eternal life. Tulip is chosen as the national flower of Iran and Turkey.

If roses are the kings of love, then during the Victorian era, when a man gave his beloved a red tulip, it meant he wanted to say, "You are so beautiful that even a fleeting glimpse of you brightens my face like fire, and my heart burns to ashes." For Persians, the tulip is a proposal from a young man to the one in his dreams. By giving a tulip, he wants to convey that "just like the red color of the flower, inside me, the flame of love is burning."
